General Questions

Working With Our Firm

Do you offer consultations?

Yes. Prospective clients can request an initial consultation to discuss their matter and possible next steps.

What should I bring to a consultation?

Bring any relevant documents, photos, reports, contracts, emails, insurance information, or notes related to your matter.

How quickly should I contact an attorney?

It is usually best to contact an attorney as soon as possible, especially if deadlines, documents, or insurance claims may be involved.

Does contacting the firm create an attorney-client relationship?

No. An attorney-client relationship is only created after proper conflict checks, agreement, and written engagement by the firm.

Personal Injury

Accidents And Injury Claims

What should I do after an accident?

Seek medical attention, document what happened, take photos if safe, gather witness information, and avoid giving detailed statements without guidance.

How long do I have to file a claim?

Deadlines vary depending on the type of claim and parties involved. Speaking with an attorney early can help protect important rights.

Do personal injury cases always go to court?

No. Many matters are resolved through negotiation or settlement, but some cases may require litigation depending on the facts.

What compensation may be available?

Depending on the case, compensation may involve medical expenses, lost income, pain and suffering, property damage, and other losses.

Estate Planning

Planning For The Future

Do I need an estate plan?

Many people benefit from having basic estate planning documents, especially if they own property, have children, or want clear instructions in place.

What documents are commonly included?

Estate plans may include wills, trusts, powers of attorney, healthcare directives, and beneficiary planning documents.

What is the difference between a will and a trust?

A will directs how assets may be distributed after death, while a trust can provide more control over asset management and transfer.

How often should an estate plan be updated?

Estate plans should be reviewed after major life events such as marriage, divorce, children, property purchases, or major financial changes.

Real Estate Law

Closings, Contracts, And Property Matters

Do I need an attorney for a real estate closing?

Many buyers and sellers choose to work with an attorney to review contracts, address issues, and help guide the closing process.

Can you review a purchase contract?

Yes. A real estate attorney can review contract terms, identify concerns, and explain obligations before signing.

What issues can delay a closing?

Common issues may include title problems, financing delays, inspection concerns, missing documents, or unresolved contract terms.

Do you help with commercial real estate?

Yes. Commercial real estate matters may involve leases, purchases, sales, contracts, financing documents, and business-related property issues.
